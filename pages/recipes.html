1. Roasted Turkey
Ingredients:
1 whole turkey (12–14 pounds)
1/4 cup olive oil or melted butter
2 teaspoons salt
1 teaspoon black pepper
1 onion, quartered
1 lemon, quartered
1 bunch fresh thyme
1 bunch fresh rosemary
4 garlic cloves, smashed
2 cups chicken broth (for basting)
Instructions:
Prep the turkey: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Remove the giblets and pat the turkey dry with paper towels.
Season the turkey: Rub the turkey all over with olive oil or melted butter. Season generously with salt and pepper. Stuff the cavity with onion, lemon, thyme, rosemary, and garlic.
Roast the turkey: Place the turkey breast-side up on a roasting rack in a large pan. Pour chicken broth into the bottom of the pan. Roast, basting occasionally with the pan juices, for about 3 to 3.5 hours (15 minutes per pound), or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F in the thickest part of the thigh.
Rest before carving: Let the turkey rest for 20 minutes before carving.

2. Creamy Mashed Potatoes
Ingredients:
2 pounds potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet)
1/2 cup butter
1/2 cup heavy cream
Salt and pepper to taste
1/4 cup sour cream (optional, for extra creaminess)
Instructions:
Cook the potatoes: Peel and cut potatoes into chunks. Boil in salted water for 15-20 minutes, or until fork-tender.
Mash the potatoes: Drain and return potatoes to the pot. Add butter, heavy cream, and sour cream (if using). Mash with a potato masher or electric mixer until smooth and creamy.
Season: Add salt and pepper to taste. Keep warm until serving.

3. Classic Stuffing
Ingredients:
1 loaf of day-old bread (cut into cubes)
1/2 cup butter
1 onion, chopped
2 celery stalks, chopped
2 garlic cloves, minced
2 cups chicken broth
1 teaspoon dried sage
1 teaspoon dried thyme
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon black pepper
1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped (optional)
1/2 cup chopped pecans or walnuts (optional)
Instructions:
Prep the bread: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread bread cubes on a baking sheet and toast for 10 minutes until dry.
Cook the vegetables: In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add onion, celery, and garlic and sauté until softened (about 5-7 minutes).
Combine: In a large bowl, mix the toasted bread cubes with the sautéed vegetables, chicken broth, sage, thyme, salt, and pepper. Add nuts and parsley if desired.
Bake: Transfer the stuffing mixture to a greased baking dish and bake for 30-40 minutes, until golden and crispy on top.

4. Homemade Cranberry Sauce
Ingredients:
12 ounces fresh or frozen cranberries
1 cup sugar
1/2 cup orange juice
1/2 teaspoon orange zest (optional)
Instructions:
Cook the cranberries: In a medium saucepan, combine cranberries, sugar, and orange juice. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to a simmer.
Simmer: Cook, stirring occasionally, for 10-15 minutes, until the cranberries burst and the sauce thickens. Add orange zest if desired.
Cool: Remove from heat and let cool to room temperature. It will thicken further as it cools.

5. Green Bean Casserole
Ingredients:
1 pound fresh green beans, trimmed (or use 2 cans of green beans)
1 can (10.5 oz) cream of mushroom soup
1/2 cup milk
1 teaspoon soy sauce (optional)
1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
1/2 teaspoon onion powder
1 1/2 cups crispy fried onions (divided)
Instructions:
Prep the green beans: If using fresh green beans, blanch them in boiling water for 4-5 minutes, then drain and set aside.
Make the sauce: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cream of mushroom soup, milk, soy sauce, garlic powder, and onion powder. Stir in half of the fried onions.
Combine and bake: Add the green beans to the soup mixture and stir to coat. Transfer to a greased baking dish. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 25 minutes.
Top and serve: Remove from the oven, stir, and top with the remaining fried onions. Return to the oven for an additional 5-10 minutes, until the onions are golden brown.

Serving Suggestions:
Turkey: Carve and serve with gravy made from the pan drippings.
Mashed potatoes: Serve with extra butter or gravy on top.
Stuffing: Pair with turkey or enjoy on its own.
Cranberry sauce: Serve alongside turkey or mashed potatoes for a tangy contrast.
Green bean casserole: A classic side to add some crunch and creaminess to the meal.
Enjoy your Thanksgiving feast!
